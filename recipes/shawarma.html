Shawarma, a cultural arabic wrap traditionally made out of multiple layers of thin bread, chicken kebab or sometimes even beef and a delicious garlic sauce. The sauce is very thick, it has the perfect balance of not beeing garlicy and the right consistency.

Some may say "You'll never be able to make a Shawarma without a kebab skewer at home!" - But the following recipe shows that you can make at least a imitation of it.

Dont get me wrong, you'll never make the meat as tasty as from the skewer but heres the trick- The sauce may be better than some restaurants. Lets get to it!

Ingredients for 6/7 Wraps

------------------------------------------------------

MEAT MARINADE

For the marinade you'll need:

  • 700g of chicken (preferably thigh)
  • 1 tbsp paprika paste
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp natural yogurt
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • 1.5 tbsp white vinegar
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp pepper
  • 1.5 tsp paprika powder
  • 1/2 tsp curry powder
  • 3/4 tsp ginger (or ginger-garlic paste)
  • 3 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp sugar

------------------------------------------------------

GARLIC SAUCE

For the perfect sauce:

  • 70g garlic cloves (about 1.5 bulbs)
  • 220ml rapeseed oil (or sunflower oil)
  • 1 to 2 pinches of salt
  • 60ml lemon juice (juice of about 1.5 lemons)
  • 1.5 tbsp cold water

------------------------------------------------------

REST / ADD-ONS

  • Cut pickles into strips (the turkish-style ones are great!)
  • 6-7 Arabic flatbreads OR 3 - 4 larger flatbreads (from your local arab market)

------------------------------------------------------

STEPS

  1. Prepare the Meat marinade

    1. Slice the chicken thighs (breasts also possible) in thin wide strips
    2. Mix all marinade ingredients (paprika paste, tomato paste, yogurt, etc.) thoroughly in a bowl
    3. Add the chicken strips and coat them well with the marinade
    4. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight, for best flavor.

  2. Make the Garlic Sauce:

    1. Place garlic cloves, oil, salt, lemon juice, and cold water in a small container
    2. Blend until the sauce is creamy and firm
    • Important advice: Start blending from the bottom of your sauce and slowly go up while the sauce is getting thicker and thicker

  3. Prepare the Extras:

    1. Cut pickles into strips
    2. Have the flatbreads ready

  4. Cooking the Chicken

    1. Neutral oil or preferably Ghee on a large pan
    2. Sear first at maximum heat
    3. When the marinade dissolves, switch to medium
    • Its important to note that if you do not have a big pan, don't throw the whole chicken in it. As it may get to saucy because of the yoghurt. Either sear it separately with 2 different pans, which I recommend or add less chicken first and then slowly more.

  5. Assemble the Shawarma Rolls:

    1. Spread garlic sauce on the flatbread. - Be generous, it's a MUST
    2. Add marinated chicken and pickles.
    3. Roll tightly and serve.
    • Another Important advice: When serving, out a spoon of garlic sauce on top of the first bite, for maximum enjoyment.
