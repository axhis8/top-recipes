The traditional pizza, in my opinion the best. Very simple, yet so delicious. Very easy to make, but it feels so rewarding when you eat it. That's why it was worth including in my TOP recipe. The process may take time, because it is important to let the dough rest. The upside: an airy dough. Thus, eating a pizza is like eating a cloud.

Personally, I don't like making food where you need to wait overnight, so I recommend making multiple doughs, freezing them, and using them over the next few months. You can look that up on Google. Now, let's get to the recipe.

This recipe has a dough of 70% hydration, ensuring a airy, easy to digest pizza. If the dough is hard to deal, you can use less water, for example like 60%. But it wont be as light and tasty as the latter. With the following ingredients, you can make up to 6 Pizzas. A scale is very much recommended for the ingredients.

Ingredients

DOUGH

  • 1kg all purpose flour
    • aim the ones with 13g protein, the more the better - so the dough doesn't break easily when you work with it
  • 700ml cold water
  • 30g salt
  • 3g fresh yeast
  • 100g sugar
    • Why sugar you may ask. It's important as it gives the color and a beautiful crust, as we won't work with a stone pizza oven. If you have one which goes up to 400Â°C, than you might skip this ingredient.
  • 5g salt per 400g tomato sauce
  • basil leaves

SAUCE

  • San Marzano peeled tomatoes without citrus
    • if it is too expensive or not available in your local grocery, then any canned peeled tomatoes without citrus should do the job.

TOPPINGS

  • 3 packs mozzarella (fresh are better than the dried ones)
  • Parmesan
  • Basil leaves
  • If you wish to add more, like tuna or onions feel free to do so (except for pineapple).

STEPS

  1. Preparing the dough:

    1. In a large bowl, combine flour, water (reserve about 100ml), yeast and sugar
    2. Knead for 5 - 10 minutes until it looks crumbly
    3. Add salt and the remaining water
    4. Cover the bowl with a cloth and let it rest for 15 minutes
    5. Fold the dough from the sides (do not knead) and let it rest for another 15 minutes covered - so the dough keeps air inside and creates tension
    6. Fold again; once the dough has a better shape, transfer it to the table from the bowl and continue folding gently there
    7. Cover the dough with the bowl downside again and let it rest for 15 minutes
    8. Repeat folding until the dough becomes smoother. Brush a little olive oil on the dough and in the bowl so it wont stick over night
    9. Place the dough in a container (preferably small) and let it rest in the refrigerator for 24 hours (minimum 8 hours, maximum 48 hours) covered.

  2. Making the sauce:

    1. Put a can (should be about 400g) in a Bowl
    2. Add 5g of salt
    3. Now get your gloves ready and smash those tomatoes. Why not a blender? Blending may destroy the tomato seeds, thus making the sauce more bitter
    4. Slap some basil leaves (literally, they deserve some spanking) - as it results more aroma
    5. Mix and leave it in the fridge covered for the next day.

  3. Preparing the toppings:

    1. Mozzarella should be prepared the day before. As stated it's better to use fresh ones than the dried one as they are overall tastier. But putting them directly on the pizza might be to watery
    2. So we cut them into strips
    3. Put it in a sieve over a bowl
    4. Then let them rest over night, so the liquid may drip on the bowl, thus still being fresh but also not to watery.

  4. The dough the day after:

    1. Take the container and flip it upside down opened over the table so the dough falls smoothly on it - shake if needed
    2. admire its size
    3. Divide the dough into portions of 280g (with a scale) for Neapolitan pizzas. One small portion will remain; you can use it to make a mini pizza or homemade brea.
    4. Place each portion in a small container or all portions in a big container and let them rest for at least 2 hours, covered with a cloth
    5. Preheat the oven to its maximum temperature (convection) at least 30 minutes before the 2 hours are up
    6. After 2 hours, sprinkle flour or preferably semolina on the dough portions to prevent sticking
    7. Sprinkle flour or preferably semolina on the work surface, place a dough portion on it, and gently stretch and shape it (do not press too hard, use fingertips carefully, go toward the edge but not completely through). You want to keep the air inside the dough
    8. Flip many times and keep shaping the dough in a round form.

  5. Baking:

    1. Remove the tray from the oven (be careful, it's hot!). Quickly place the pizza on parchment paper, then put the paper with the pizza om it carefully on the tray
    2. Spread tomato sauce on the pizza and slide it into the oven
    3. Bake for 4 - 8 minutes (depending on the oven) until the dough is firm and the sauce is not burnt
    4. Add toppings: first Parmesan, then basil, and finally Mozzarella at the end.
    5. Return the pizza to the oven for 2 - 6 minutes (depending on the oven)
    6. Be careful that the mozzarella wont burn and keep an eye on the pizza
    7. Remove, slice, and enjoy! Repeat the process for the remaining pizzas. A good pizza can be pulled away with the hands (Cornicione-Test) has a dark bottom and an airy, raised edge.
